Vegetable Soup

This vegetable soup, a family recipe, is delicious and easy to prepare.

  • Ingredients:

  • 1 Tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion
  • 6–8 cloves garlic
  • 7 red potatoes
  • 7 carrots
  • 6-oz can tomato paste
  • 15-oz can garbanzo beans
  • 1 qt vegetable broth
  • 6–10 cups water
  • 2-3 full stems of fresh basil leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Optional: Parmesan to top
  • Optional: Sliced black olives to top

Dice the onion and mince the garlic. In a large pot over medium-low heat, saute the onion and garlic in the olive oil until translucent and fragrant. While the aromatics are cooking, chop carrots into rounds, slicing any that are more than an inch thick in half lengthwise first. Add the carrots to the pot and stir. Dice the potatoes into half-inch cubes and add to the pot. Cook, stirring occasionally, for 10–15 minutes to allow root vegetables to begin to soften.

Once the potatoes are just beginning to be noticeably soft around the edges, add the garbanzo beans, tomato paste, vegetable broth, 6 cups water, and salt and pepper to taste. Stir, cover the pot, and turn the heat up to high until the pot boils. Reduce the heat to a simmer and let cook, uncovered, for 30–60 minutes, testing potatoes with a fork every 15 minutes.

When the potatoes are fully tender, roughly chop and add the fresh basil. If the broth has cooked down more than desired, add up to 4 cups more water until broth regains desired consistency. Allow the soup to continue simmering for 5 minutes after the basil is added, then remove from the heat. Serve warm with parmesan and sliced olives to top and a thick slice of bread on the side.
